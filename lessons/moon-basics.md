# Author Notes

This lesson should help a child understand:

- the moon does not make its own light
- sunlight lights the moon
- the moon looks different because we see different amounts of the lit half
- the moon is still round even when it looks like a crescent

Keep the language concrete and visual. Use simple sky examples and avoid technical jargon.

```teachbox-step
{
  "step_id": "intro-1",
  "type": "narration",
  "title": "The moon does not glow by itself",
  "script_chunks": [
    "Tonight we are learning why the moon can look different on different nights.",
    "The moon does not make its own light.",
    "The sun shines on the moon, and that is what makes the moon look bright to us."
  ]
}
```

```teachbox-step
{
  "step_id": "example-1",
  "type": "narration",
  "title": "Seeing the bright part",
  "script_chunks": [
    "Only one half of the moon is lit by the sun at a time.",
    "But from Earth, we do not always see the same amount of that bright half.",
    "Some nights we see a little bright piece, and some nights we see a lot more."
  ]
}
```
